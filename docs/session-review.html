Why it exists

A grill stuck cold, and no way to see inside it.

The build started as troubleshooting: the grill wasn't coming up to temp, and the official app gave almost nothing to work with. Diagnosing it meant building an app that shows the whole picture — and keeps it.

The wall

Almost no data

The official app shows a temperature and hides the rest — no auger or fan state, no live curve, no record of the cook. Hard to tell a dying firepot from a bad reading. (And our own settings were quietly reverting — a bug that, fittingly, also hid the truth.)

The answer

Show it, record it, watch it

Every reading on screen and written to the cook — grill and setpoint, each probe, and the auger, fan and igniter as they fire. Sessions are labeled by what you're cooking and replayable. Warnings fire before you'd notice. State is legible, by design.

Root cause · a real failure

Why the grill wouldn't heat: it had a fire.

01 · triggerImproper shutdown

Powered off straight from high heat — with no cool-down to 200° first.

→
02 · fireUp the auger tube

Fire crept back up the pellet-tube assembly toward the hopper.

→
03 · damageBushing melts

A plastic bushing in the auger housing melted from the heat.

→
04 · failureMotor seizes

The auger motor jammed and was damaged — it never turned, so no pellets, no heat.

The fix became the feature. Teardown confirmed the auger motor never ran; it had to be replaced. The graceful shutdown below enforces the exact cool-to-200° step that would have prevented this — it exists because of this failure, not in spite of a hypothetical one.

The centerpiece · fire prevention

Turning off a hot grill, safely.

Pressing "Turn Off" no longer cuts power at whatever temperature the grill is running. It runs a state machine — orchestrated in the main process so closing the window can't strand it.

Phase 01 · Cooling

Ramp to 200°F

Sets the grill to 200° and shows live progress: "cooling to 200°: 320° now (58% cooled)".

→temp ≤ 210°
send off
Phase 02 · Finishing

Fan cool-down

Powers off; the controller's fan keeps running to fully cool the firepot.

→module off
+ fan stopped
Phase 03 · Concluded

Powered off

"Your Pit Boss is powered off — the grill is cool and this session is concluded."

Second Turn Off press — skips the cool-down and powers off now.
Pick a new temperature — cancels the shutdown ("keep cooking").
30-minute watchdog — warns if the cool-down never finishes.
⚠

Why it matters: this is the failure that started the project. Shutting the grill off straight from high heat let the fire creep up the auger tube — a melted bushing seized the motor, and it needed replacing. Cooling to 200° first, and confirming the fan cycle finished, is the step that prevents it.

Warnings · the readout

Every warning has a rule you can tune.

These are the walk-away warnings — the app watches so you don't have to, and each fires as a desktop notification and in the status bar. One tunable rule apiece.

SignalFires when
Lid / door opengrill temp drops ≥ 25°/min AND ≥ 20° below setpoint
Out of pellets≥ 4°/min decline over ~4 min AND ≥ 40° below setpoint
Probe over targetreading ≥ 5° past the probe's set target
Grease-fire flare-upgrill reads ≥ 100° above setpoint while running
Cleaning due5 cooks · 30 h of use · 3 flare-ups since last clean
Graceful shutdownabove 250° → cool to 200° → power off at 210°

First-pass thresholds — gathered in one block per engine and being calibrated against the real grill right now.

And it flushed out three fixes a simulation never would: a flare-up false-alarm during the cool-down (200° setpoint vs. a still-328° grill), a probe-target clear that left its warning up, and alert visibility (12s in the status bar is easy to miss at the grill).
